Full Biography

David E. Saunders

Dave Saunders is a retired United States Marine Corps officer and the founder of ClearPath Strategies. Over more than two decades of service, he built a career at the intersection of strategic planning, organizational leadership, and complex problem solving — working across some of the most demanding environments in the world.

Dave served at the highest levels of Marine Corps leadership, including as Deputy of the Commandant of the Marine Corps Action Group — a small team responsible for providing holistic policy and strategy recommendations directly to the Commandant. He led strategy and force design analysis at Headquarters Marine Corps for over three years, translating national security documents into actionable Marine Corps guidance and leading a ten-person team of senior analysts. Earlier in his career, he served as a Future Operations Planner for III Marine Expeditionary Force in Okinawa, Japan, leading contingency and crisis planning for an organization of over 30,000 personnel across the Pacific.

His operational experience includes a combat deployment to Afghanistan, where he orchestrated the closure of an austere forward operating base — coordinating the simultaneous drawdown of 3,000 personnel, $65 million in equipment, remediation of 320 acres of land, and the demolition of 15 structures. He also led the military's COVID response planning on Okinawa in early 2020, standing up a joint crisis team from scratch before most organizations had begun to respond.

Dave has worked extensively in multi-national and interagency environments, including bilateral engagements with the Japanese Ministry of Defense and Foreign Affairs, multi-national planning exercises in the Philippines, and most recently as an adjunct professor at the Saudi Arabian National Guard's Command and General Staff College in Riyadh, where he taught future military leaders to use history as a framework for critical thinking.

He holds a Bachelor of Science in Civil Engineering from Cornell University, a Master of Arts in International Relations from Webster University, a Master of Arts in Operational Studies from the Marine Corps School of Advanced Warfighting, and a Master of Arts in National Security Studies from the U.S. Army War College. He is an Olmsted Scholar — a highly selective fellowship that placed him at Mohammed V University in Rabat, Morocco for deep cultural and language immersion — and a fellow of MIT's Seminar XXI national security program. He holds an active Top Secret security clearance and has conversational Arabic.

Dave lives on Cape Cod with his wife and children. He founded ClearPath to make the problem-solving disciplines of military strategic planning available to the nonprofits, startups, and mission-driven organizations that need them most.
